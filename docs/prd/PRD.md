**Product Requirements Document: Project Module**

### **Personas**

**Outside rental rep**
They physically walk **projects** to log new subcontractors, estimate future equipment needs (like how many boom lifts are required), and complete Planned Annual Rate (PAR) visit activities. They care about having a blazing-fast, mobile-friendly interface—ideally optimized for an iPad—that lets them drop location pins and capture notes quickly without resorting to Google Maps or offline spreadsheets.

**Outside machine sales rep (MSR)**
They monitor **projects** to determine which contractors have won bids and whether those winners will deploy their existing fleet or need to purchase new equipment. They care about coordinating with the rental and product support teams on the same **project** to avoid turf wars and ensure the dealership maximizes its overall equipment sales.

**Outside product support sales rep (PSSR)**
They take over **project** relationships during the later phases of a **project** to secure ongoing maintenance agreements and service the machines operating in the field. They care deeply about unit-level location tracking—knowing exactly where a specific asset is parked on a sprawling **project**—so their field mechanics can find and repair equipment efficiently.

**Outside power systems sales rep**
They manage the installation of highly specialized, stationary equipment, such as standby generator sets, on large-scale construction **projects** like dams or hospitals. They care about granular, sub-**project** tracking (e.g., knowing Unit 1 is on the 20th floor of Building A) and coordinating their complex installations seamlessly alongside the general construction teams.

**Inside sales rep (ISR)**
They support the outside sales teams by using the "Customer Search" app to look up **project** details and verify subcontractor accounts when taking inbound calls or quoting equipment. *(Drawing on your external insight)* They care about having a centralized, monetized CRM that instantly surfaces accurate **project** contacts and hierarchies so they can process quotes quickly and provide seamless customer service without friction.

**Parts counter staff**
They also use the "Customer Search" app to verify details, but specifically use the CRM to coordinate parts drop-offs and dispatch field mechanics to active construction **projects**. They care about having precise, unit-level location details and specific gate-entry notes so that delivery drivers and technicians don't waste time getting lost on massive, multi-acre **projects**.

**Sales Manager**
They review their team's **project** PAR compliance and analyze the aggregated revenue pipeline for all active construction **projects** in their territory. They care about eliminating "pipeline inflation"—which happens when multiple contractors bid on the same job and artificially inflate the forecast—so they can accurately predict revenue and hit their budgets.

**VP of Sales**
They dictate the overarching sales strategy and monitor the dealership’s penetration into massive, multi-year megaprojects. They care about driving high CRM adoption to eliminate "shadow IT" (like reps going rogue with personal spreadsheets) and ensuring the dealership extracts the maximum share of wallet across all divisions converging on a single **project**.

**Sales Administrator**
They configure the CRM to match the dealership's unique workflow by customizing default **project** statuses and building detailed dropdown lists for specific subcontractor roles. They care about making the system as intuitive and tailored as possible so that field reps aren't forced to manually type out trades (e.g., easily selecting an "exterior skin" contractor versus a "roofer").

**System Administrator**
They manage the backend architecture and integrations, such as connecting the CRM to external intelligence feeds like the Dodge Construction Network. They care about maintaining clean database hierarchies and ensuring that automated **project** feeds don't flood the system with "noise" that would cause reps to lose faith in the data.

### **Business Imperative**

**The heavy equipment market is undergoing a massive structural shift toward "usership" over "ownership," driving record rental penetration amidst a boom in infrastructure and data center construction**. At the same time, OEMs like Caterpillar are actively mandating that their dealerships double their services and rental revenue.

Despite these massive revenue opportunities, **dealerships are operating with a critical technological blind spot because standard CRMs are built on an "Account-centric" architecture that fundamentally fails to capture the "Project-centric" reality of the rental business**. On a modern **project**, multiple accounts (General Contractors and various Subcontractors) converge simultaneously, creating a complex "many-to-many" relationship. Because current software cannot map this ecosystem, rental teams suffer from "invisible opportunities" and are forced into inefficient workarounds. Currently, reps abandon our CRM entirely to manage their territories using offline spreadsheets, Google Maps, or expensive third-party competitor platforms like Salesforce.

**Building a native Projects module is a strategic imperative to defend against Tier-1 ERP consolidation threats (like Microsoft Dynamics) and to deepen our stickiness within dealerships, thereby increasing our Gross Retention Rate (GRR) and Net Retention Rate (NRR)**. Furthermore, executing on your strategy to migrate Inside Sales Reps and Parts Counter Staff away from the free "Customer Search" app and into the core CRM (insight from outside the provided sources) will directly drive up monetized MAUs and increase overall customer spend with us.

### **Expected Outcome**

* **Explosive MAU Growth and Increased Spend:** We will successfully capture and onboard the outside rental representatives who currently refuse to use the CRM. Concurrently, migrating the daily search workflows of Inside Sales and Parts Counter staff into the main application will convert non-monetized users into billable MAUs (as per your external strategic insight).
* **Elimination of Shadow IT and Competitor Tools:** Dealerships will abandon fragmented workarounds like Google Maps, manual spreadsheets, and expensive, disconnected third-party CRMs (e.g., Salesforce), consolidating their entire sales operation onto our unified platform.
* **Accurate Pipeline Visibility and Forecasting:** Sales Managers will be able to view and calculate total pipeline revenue dynamically at the **project** level. This will solve the long-standing issue of pipeline inflation caused by multiple subcontractors bidding on the same **project**, allowing for highly accurate revenue forecasting.
* **Maximized Share of Wallet via Cross-Divisional Collaboration:** The CRM will serve as the single source of truth for every active construction **project**. This will allow Rental, Machine Sales, Product Support, and Power Systems divisions to coordinate their efforts, share **project**-specific intelligence, accurately locate assets for field mechanics, and maximize the dealership's total revenue extraction on every **project**.
